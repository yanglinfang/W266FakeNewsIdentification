FRANKFURT (Reuters) - Deutsche Bank AG expects to see one-off costs in the third quarter linked to its job cuts, litigation expenses and the reduction of risk-weighted assets, Co-Chief Executive Anshu Jain told a financial conference.	1	1	1		3
Jain reiterated that Germany's largest bank had seen solid performance in the third quarter, particularly in investment banking.	1	2	1		3
"On the revenue side we are in line, but we definitely have headwinds on the cost side," Jain told the conference in London, which was monitored over the Internet.	2	2	2		3
To free up equity capital, Deutsche plans to slash risk weighted assets by about 90 billion euros ($117 billion) by the end of the first quarter 2013.	1	1	1		3
Deutsche reckons with costs of around half a billion euros as a result, Jain said.	1	1	1		3
Germany's flagship lender plans to cut more than 1,900 jobs, mainly in investment banking and is also battling law suits by investors and regulators.	1	1	3		3
