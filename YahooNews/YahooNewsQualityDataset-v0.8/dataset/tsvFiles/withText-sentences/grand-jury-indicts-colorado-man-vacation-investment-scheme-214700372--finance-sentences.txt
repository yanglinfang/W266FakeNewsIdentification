Grand Jury Indicts Colorado Man on Vacation Investment Scheme	1	1	1		4
According to Denver District Attorney Mitchell R. Morrissey, a grand jury has indicted a man today of using more than $8 million of investor money for his personal use.	2	1	2		4
Here are the details.	1	1	1		4
* A Denver Grand Jury returned a 20-count indictment against Brian Wellens, 42, the district attorney stated.	2	1	2		4
Wellens was charged with violating the Colorado Organized Crime Control Act, two counts of conspiracy to commit securities fraud and 17 counts of securities fraud.	1	1	3		4
* An arrest warrant has been issued for Wellens.	1	1	2		4
* Morrissey stated that the indictment was the result of a joint investigation with the Colorado Division of Securities.	2	1	1		4
* According to the charges, Wellens allegedly took approximately $8.5 million from 19 different investors between 2006 and 2011, promoting vacation investment through his company, Continental Resort Homes.	2	1	2		4
* Instead of using the investment money to purchase vacation properties, the district attorney reported, Wellens allegedly used the money for his personal expenses and other businesses.	2	1	2		4
* According to a November, 2011, report in the Colorado Springs Gazette , the Colorado Division of Securities began investigating Wellens after he filed a Chapter 7 bankruptcy listing $36.4 million in liabilities owed to 76 creditors and $2.08 million in assets.	2	1	2		4
* Wellens stated during a creditors' meeting that he took more than $2 million from an individual in order to build a vacation home in Mexico, the Gazette reported.	2	1	1		4
However, he never acquired the site or started construction on that home.	2	1	2		4
* According to the Gazette, some of the creditors in his bankruptcy included state House Majority Leader Amy Stephens (R-Monument); state Rep. Bob Gardner (R-Colorado Springs); Colorado Lottery Director Abel Tapia; Vladamir Jones Chairman Nechie Hall and former Colorado Springs entrepreneur Ron Muns.	2	1	1		4
* The violation to the Colorado Organized Crime Control Act is a class 2 felony which carries a penalty for up to 48 years in prison and up to $1 million in fines, according to the Felony Guide for Colorado.	2	1	2		4
The conspiracy charges are class 4 felonies, calling for two to 12 years in prison and up to $500,000 in fines.	1	1	2		4
The securities fraud charges are class 3 felonies, punishable by up to 24 years in prison and up to $750,000 dollars in fines.	1	1	2		4
